The problem

Visa research is a maze.

Whether you can visit, study or work in another country depends on your nationality, your age, the destination, the visa type, your funds, your documents — and official rules that are scattered across government websites, worded differently everywhere, and changing all the time. Most people end up piecing it together from forums and guesswork.

🌍

Rules depend on everything

The same destination can mean a free entry, an online authorisation, or a full visa application — depending only on your passport.

📄

Requirements hide in fine print

Funds thresholds, age bands, quotas, ballots, insurance, sponsor letters — each lives on a different official page.

⏳

Everything changes

Fees, quotas and opening dates shift every season. What was true last year may be wrong today.

The idea

One profile. Every path, side by side.

Wayfare takes a short profile — nationality, age, education, savings, goals — and maps it against researched visa rules for each destination. Instead of twenty browser tabs, you get a single comparable view of which visa paths look possible, which look partial, and exactly which requirements you'd still need to verify.

How it works

Five steps, no jargon.

1

Enter your profile

Nationality, age, education, English level, savings — nothing is stored; it's a prototype.

2

Choose a destination

Pick a country on the interactive globe to explore what it offers someone like you.

3

See possible visa options

Visitor, student, working holiday and more — scored as eligible, partial or unlikely for your profile.

4

Review the details

Each option lists warnings, requirements, fees and conditions drawn from official-source research.

5

Confirm on official sites

Every result points you back to the official government source — that's always the final word.

Other destinations shown in the prototype use simulated placeholder rules and are clearly marked as such. Wayfare does not claim global coverage — audited destinations grow over time.

Trust & safety

Honest by design.

  • ✓Official-source research. Audited rules are captured from government immigration websites — never from blogs, agents or forums.
  • ✓Warnings over promises. Anything that can't be verified from your profile — funds, documents, quotas, ballots — is shown as a warning or checklist item, not a green light.
  • ✓Time-sensitive facts are flagged. Fees, quotas and opening dates change; Wayfare marks them and tells you to re-check the official page.
  • ✓No approval guarantees. Wayfare never predicts that a visa will be granted — only whether the published requirements look like a fit.
  • ✓Not legal advice. Wayfare is an eligibility simulator. It does not replace official government guidance or professional immigration advice.

Where this is going

Making mobility understandable.

The goal is simple: help travellers understand their realistic visa options, cut through the confusion of scattered official pages, and make immigration information easy to compare — building step by step toward a reliable public beta covering the world's most in-demand destinations.

A note before you try it: this is an early prototype. Some countries and visa types are still being added, and non-audited destinations use simulated rules. Whatever Wayfare shows you, always confirm the details on the official government website before making plans.
